The Passenger

I had been in a wave pool
we were visiting
barely large enough to fit
the full-size man I’m not

still I kicked against it
knowing I’d be pushed back
the memory of someone
who has died no different

in my mind and Wanda
she’d be very old now too

I had been learning
my body and liked
most of all to be alone
with it new places

my hair stiffed with
chlorine when we left
cold on the journey
the wet road a gyroscope

it happens when you
briefly close your eyes
