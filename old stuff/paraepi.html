Parataxis / Epistaxis

... a maddening title, but to my mind the simplest and most accurate... the first word refers to a technique in poetry in which images or fragments, usually starkly dissimilar, are juxtaposed without a clear connection, while the second word simply means "nosebleed"

There was a place in the copse
We did not call a clearing
And when Dad sells the house we know
We will not return here

And a poem in the new book is blue
And at work the cyan cartridge leaks
And a drop of acid on a flame
Creates a brilliant spike of color

And driving out of town I bled
And quickly cooled and turned obsidian
