This is What Brothers Do

In my varsity dream I’m captain
and you’re the one kissing my cleats.

I look over the ledge of a stiff hill
from the San Bernardino Mission and,
rotating courtside, you push two season tickets
to the best show in town to my chest—
Our bows grow fast & strong here, where the hurricane hits
hard and in circles around my body.

My belly aches and I know you can’t make it better—
I’m focused on the game,
hands leave the trap,
throw the pig through my legs,
palms clammed up with an excitement
or a frost that makes them close tight with fear—
I can’t quite feel the tips of my fingers but
it’s midsummer and ninety-some-odd degrees
in the shade.

You tell me your dad’s a good time pharaoh but that
he and your mom are two doors next to each other
that open to different rooms.
You dribble a little and say,
       Remember when we were in high school
       we used to laugh & kiss at the same time?
Growing the greenbelt feels impossible
but the summer just started and before you know it
we’ll both jump the fence into our own private mysteries.

Cupid draws his bow and shoots a thick arrow into the shadows,
far back in the darkest part of the park.
It’s Ten Pee Em and so hot I can feel a little dew in my brow—
There’s a lot of heat here for us
on the edge of the basketball court.

Tomorrow I’ll have a lap around the middle school,
take off my shirt while I’m still running
and tie it around my neck—
I don’t feel how hot the sun is
until it’s not there anymore.

There are so many things I have to do but
I am waiting for you to get here.
You are far away right now but
I can hold you if I want—
I can taste you if I open my mouth wide
enough.
